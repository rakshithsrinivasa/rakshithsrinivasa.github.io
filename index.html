About Me

I am a senior machine learning researcher at Samsung Research America, Mountain View, CA.

My research interests include multimodal LLMs, cross-modal representation alignment and efficient inference. I am also interested in efficient algorithms for linear algebraic computations and in using tools from randomized linear algebra to solve optimization problems.

I obtained my Ph.D. from Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta in 2020. My advisor was Dr. Justin Romberg. I also worked closely with Dr. Mark Davenport. During my Ph.D., I worked on subspace learning and sketching methods for high-dimensional linear algebra for applications in array signal processing. Following my Ph.D., I was a research scientist at IQVIA, where I worked on graph neural networks and deep learning based ranking methods for applications in clinical trial patient recruitment and prediction of COVID-19 spread. I joined Samsung Research America as a senior ML researcher in 2021, where I have been working on multimodal learning, on-device AI, and LLM based language translation.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing tennis (and most other racket sports), hiking, playing scrabble and cooking. I enjoy running and I have completed the San Francisco Marathon in 2023 and I am currently working on improving my timing. I am also a dance enthusiast and enjoy latin dacing, including salsa and bachata.
